## Positioning the agent

A customer-support agent is more than an FAQ bot: it must **plug into your stack** (ticketing, CRM, knowledge base) and enforce guardrails on sensitive actions (refunds, contractual commitments).

## What pays off quickly

Early wins usually come from:

- Assisted replies for human agents (copilot)
- **Macro responses** personalized from the customer record
- Smart routing to the right team

## Risks to plan for

Without oversight, models can **hallucinate** policies or dates. You need controls: mandatory sources, explicit refusal outside scope, decision logging.

## Link to back-office automation

When support handles billing disputes, align the knowledge base with workflows documented in projects like [invoice automation](/blog/automatiser-facturation-ia). For model/data architecture, cross-read our [RAG vs fine-tuning](/blog/rag-vs-fine-tuning) article.

## Conclusion

ROI hinges on **handling time** and **satisfaction** — measure them before expanding automation scope.
